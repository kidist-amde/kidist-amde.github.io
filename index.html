About Me

Greetings! My name is Kidist, a recent MSc Data Science graduate from the University of Trento, Italy. I have a solid foundation in programming languages, statistical methods, and machine learning frameworks. I have earned two Master's degrees in Machine Intelligence and Data Science, as well as a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science. I am enthusiastic about continuous learning, having pursued various online courses, and AI events and talks such as conferences, workshops, summits, and hackathons to further my expertise in the fields of AI and Data Science.

My research interest lies in Generative AI and multimodal learning (vision and language), specifically in the context of social good. My main objective is on promoting positive outcomes, such as explainability, fairness, robustness, scalability, privacy, safety, and ethics, to ensure accountability and transparency in AI development.

Ph.D. Research Interest Area

My research interest centers around creating explainable deep learning models that tackle biases towards race and gender in generative models. Additionally, I'm intrigued by exploring responsible practices when working with generative AI, in both large language models (LLMs) and image generation. I aim to examine the biases inherent in GANs and diffusion models, as well as the tendency of state-of-the-art LLMs to prioritize increasing the number of parameters and training data without transparency or discretion in data ingestion. Ultimately, my objective is to pinpoint responsible practices for working with generative AI, with a particular emphasis on addressing issues related to bias and transparency in GenAI.
